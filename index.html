The path that brought me here isn't the trajectory anyone would have predicted.

When my school grades weren't enough, I didn't wait around. I chose to move forward and started studying advertising and PR at a small college in Korea. Since then, I have always stepped into difficult and uncertain environments. I volunteered for the Special Forces, moved to the University of Illinois to finish my degree, and, while still a student, cold-emailed my way into a fashion house to create an internship that didn't even exist and became its very first intern.

My career since has been simple. I introduce new types of products to new markets and build the systems to make them work. I managed a global mobile account for a major tech company at a PR firm, coordinating thirteen country offices. Later, I joined a marine biotech startup to build its early distribution and logistics network. This combination of handling technology and raw logistics brought me to Google's retail operations in Korea, where I introduced Chromecast to the local market for the first time.

I like opening new territories. I volunteered for a project in India and built the local field infrastructure from scratch for Google's first smartphone, the Pixel. Later, I founded a D2C skincare startup. I managed everything from developing five products to building new sales channels, which gave me real world experience in running a business and managing financial risks.

After closing the startup, I returned to Google's retail team to optimize our channels, while also supporting product launches in Taiwan and Thailand. This experience of growing multi-country markets led me to join Smollan, Google's regional partnering agency. I relocated to Singapore to manage the regional Google hardware account.

For the past several years, I have managed retail operations across six APAC markets, looking after over 2,000 stores and a field team of more than 100 people. Along the way, I completed my MBA at the National University of Singapore to review and test my ten years of hands-on experience.

The industries looked completely different, from shellfish to cosmetics to global tech giants. But the core of my work never changed. Read a new environment fast. Launch a new category. Build infrastructure where there is no playbook. Expand the channel.

Today, I am opening new markets and testing new solutions where AI meets physical retail. There is no manual for this yet, which is exactly why I am here.
